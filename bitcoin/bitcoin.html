Bitcoin: The Gold Standard for Cryptocurrency

History of Bitcoin

Bitcoin was the world's first decentralized currency. Created by a man named Satoshi Nakamoto, who released an article online where he detailed his new creation around November, 2008. A few months later the Bitcoin network came online, with Satoshi himself mining the "Genesis" block. For nearly a year nothing much happened in the space of Cryptocurrency.Throughout 2010, Bitcoin gained steam, with ups and downs along the way. By the beginning of 2011, the value of Bitcoin is roughly a dollar. Then, for about 2 years, the Bitcoin universe was relatively quiet. The Bitcoin price shot up after the US Senate held hearings on it. Popularity in China, propelled the price to over $1100/BTC. The price of BTC (Bitcoin) crashed from that quick high and dropped to roughly $210/BTC at one point last year. However, it has since recovered and today, Bitcoin is stronger and more stable than ever. The current price is $420/BTC.

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is the gold standard for Cryptocurrency today. As the adoption rate has increased, the price has become more stable. Cryptocurrencies have three huge benefits over regular currency.

  1. Fast: Bitcoin transactions take just minutes and allow you to send coins anywhere in the world with just an internet connection.
  2. Secure: Bitcoins are pseudonymous which means that you name isn't tied to the coins. You aren't completely anonymous because transferring coins leave a traceable trail on the Blockchain.
  3. Freedom: You control your own money and no bank or government can tax your coins.
