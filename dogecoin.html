Dogecoin: A fun introduction to Cryptocurrency

A Crypto for the People

Chances are you've heard of Dogecoin, most notably if you watched the Winter Olympics in Feburary, 2014. Redditors, members of an online form called Reddit, donated Cryptocurrency in order to fund the Jamaican Bobsled Team. Dogecoin follows in Bitcoin's creation using the same technologies like the Blockchain to create another cryptocurrency. Dogecoin also helped fund a NASCAR driver, Josh Wise. What started as a silly internet meme, turned into a serious Cryptocurrency devoted to the community. Dogecoin hasn't experienced the kind of growth Bitcoin has, but it remains a powerful influence in the crypto community.

Why Dogecoin?

Bitcoin is in fact the king of the Cryptocurrency world, but there are hundreds of so called Altcoins. Dogecoin is one of the largest alts, but it's value is significantly less than Bitcoin. There's a very good reason for this however, the cheaper price of Dogecoin makes it very easy to buy lots of coins, and the supply of Dogecoins is much higher than Bitcoin. The currency is a lot more accessible in this way.

Alt Coins

Altcoins are similar to Bitcoin, but they change how their coin works. Some altcoins improve transaction privacy, others change the technologies including different ways of mining coins or improving the base code. They can do this because Bitcoin is open-source, meaning anyone can use and reuse Bitcoin's fundamental code. Some famous altcoins include: Litecoin, Peercoin, and Dogecoin.
